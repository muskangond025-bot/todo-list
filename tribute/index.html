Narendra Modi

SYNOPSIS

Narendra Modi grew up in the town of Vadnagar in India, the son of a street merchant. He entered politics as a youth and quickly rose through the ranks of Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh, a Hindu nationalist political party. Modi later joined the mainstream Bharatiya Janata Party in 1987, eventually becoming national secretary. In 2014 he was elected prime minister of India.

Background

Narendra Modi was born in the small town of Vadnagar, in northern Gujarat, India. His father was a street merchant who struggled to support the family. Young Narendra and his brother sold tea near a bus terminal to help out. Though an average student in school, Modi spent hours in the library and was known as a strong debater. In his early teens, he joined Akhil Bharatiya Vidyarthi Parishad, the student wing of Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS).

Life Dedicated to Politics

Modi had an arranged married at 18 but spent little time with his bride. He dedicated his life to politics in Gujarat, joining the RSS in 1971. During the 1975-77 political crisis, Prime Minister Indira Gandhi declared a state of emergency. Modi went underground and wrote a book, Sangharsh ma Gujarat. Gulbarg Massacre and Alleged Complicity

In February 2002, while Modi served as chief minister of Gujarat, a train was attacked, allegedly by Muslims. Violence spread, and Modi was accused of allowing the killings, but after investigations, the [Indian Supreme Court](https://main.sci.gov.in) concluded there was no evidence Modi was at fault. He was reelected chief minister in 2007 and 2012, focusing on economic growth and privatization.

Elected Prime Minister

In May 2014, he and his party were victorious, taking 282 of the 534 seats in the Lok Sabha. On May 26, 2014, Modi was sworn in as the 14th prime minister of India and the first to have been born after the country got its independence.

Policy

Since becoming prime minister, Modi has encouraged foreign businesses to invest via "Make in India". In 2014 he launched a "Clean India" campaign focusing on sanitation. In terms of foreign policy, he aligned himself with the United States, China, Japan, and Russia to improve economic ties. He has fostered diplomatic ties with many nations, although he has maintained a firm stance on security.
